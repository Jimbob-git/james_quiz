5 Facts about Sydney

1. One of the World's most deadly spiders resides in Sydney, the Sydney funnel web spider which can kill a man within 15 minutes. It's fangs are so strong they can bite through gloves or even fingernails.

2. Sydney is home to the oldest annual sailing regatta in the World, the Australia Day Regatta, which first took place in 1837

3. Sydney Opera House has lots of light bulbs, in fact in a year, 15,500 light bulbs are changed!

4. Sydney Harbour Bridge was first opened on 19 March, 1932, by Francis De Groot, a retired cavalry officer. It is the widest long span bridge and the tallest steel arch bridge in the World.

5. Sydney's popular cliff-top coastal Coogee to Bondi walk, which travels from Coogee beach, through Clovelly beach, Bronte beach, Tamarama beach and onto Bondi beach is 6 kilometers and takes about 2 hours to complete. In fact, it's more like 2.5/3 hours, I know, I've done it many times!
